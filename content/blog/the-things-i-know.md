# The Things I Know
All I know is to work hard for what I have
My parents did that in offices densely packed and cubiculed bureaus
Their parents did it in fields and bushes under scorching suns and beating downpours

All I know is to hold it in and work it out
My father did it all his life, and so did his father
Shoulder responsibility and brace on because it never is not for me

All I know is to pray when it is beyond me
My mother taught me to do so every day, and so did her mother before she left for higher education.
On my knees with head abow and her hands on my head she blessed my every step
She prayed that the hands that worked stayed clean and fed

All I know bewilders me, fills me with an inadequacy that consumes me
All I know annoys me, enrages me. Why can't you see what I see yet
All I know is staring through a window without looking at the door

All I know is what I learned, flawed yet earnest
Prejudiced and sometimes proudly ignorant
All I know is not enough, it's the great lesson in humility
So forgive me for the things I know
